## 🛂 Visa Information for Indian Citizens

### E-Visa (Recommended)
- **Type:** Tourist E-Visa
- **Validity:** 90 days from issue date
- **Stay Duration:** 30 days (single entry) or 30 days (multiple entry)
- **Processing Time:** 5-7 business days
- **Cost:** ~USD 25 (single entry) / USD 60 (multiple entry)
- **Application:** https://visa2egypt.gov.eg

**Required Documents:**
1. Passport valid for at least 6 months
2. Recent passport-size photograph
3. Return flight tickets
4. Hotel booking confirmation
5. Credit/debit card for payment

**Alternative:** Visa on Arrival
- Available at Cairo International Airport
- Cost: USD 25
- Same requirements as E-Visa
- May involve longer queues

**Important Notes:**
- Apply for E-Visa at least 7 days before travel
- Print E-Visa confirmation to show at immigration
- Keep passport copies and visa documents safe

## 📝 Important Tips & Recommendations

### Money Matters
- **Currency:** Egyptian Pound (EGP)
- **Exchange Rate:** 1 USD ≈ 30-31 EGP
- Carry USD for easier exchange
- ATMs widely available in cities
- Credit cards accepted at hotels and major restaurants
- Keep small bills for tips (baksheesh)

### Tipping Guide
- Tour guides: USD 5-10 per day
- Drivers: USD 3-5 per day
- Restaurant servers: 10-15% of bill
- Hotel staff: USD 1-2 per service

### Health & Safety
- Drink only bottled water
- Avoid street food initially
- Carry basic medications
- Travel insurance mandatory
- Keep copies of important documents
- Register with Indian Embassy in Cairo

### Cultural Etiquette
- Dress modestly at religious sites
- Remove shoes when entering mosques
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Bargain at markets (start at 50% of asking price)
- Learn basic Arabic phrases (Shukran = Thank you)

### What to Pack
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Light, breathable clothing
- Modest clothing for temples
- Swimwear for Hurghada
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
- Hat and sunglasses
- Power adapter (Type C/F, 220V)
- Camera with extra batteries
- Small backpack for day trips

### Photography
- Pyramids: Free to photograph outside
- Museums: Camera fee required (no flash)
- Tombs: Photography often prohibited
- Respect "No Photography" signs

### Best Time for Activities
- Pyramids: Early morning (7-9 AM) to avoid crowds and heat
- Temples: Morning or late afternoon for best lighting
- Nile Cruise: November is perfect weather
- Hurghada: Anytime, but morning for water activities
