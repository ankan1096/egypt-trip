🛂 Visa Information for Indian Citizens

E-Visa (Recommended)

  • Type: Tourist E-Visa
  • Cost: USD 25 (single entry) / USD 60 (multiple entry)
  • Processing: 5-7 business days
  • Validity: 90 days from issue
  • Stay: 30 days
  • Apply: visa2egypt.gov.eg

Required Documents

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months
  • Recent passport-size photograph
  • Return flight tickets
  • Hotel booking confirmation
  • Credit/debit card for payment
Important: Apply for E-Visa at least 7 days before travel. Print confirmation to show at immigration.

Morning: Optional visit to Saqqara & Memphis, transfer to airport

Afternoon: Flight to Luxor, check-in to Nile Cruise ship

Evening: Visit illuminated Luxor Temple, dinner on cruise

Luxor West Bank

Morning: Valley of the Kings, Temple of Hatshepsut, Colossi of Memnon

Afternoon: Cruise sails towards Edfu, relax on sun deck

Evening: Egyptian folklore show, overnight sailing

Cost: USD 33 per person

Edfu & Kom Ombo

Morning: Edfu Temple (best-preserved temple), horse carriage ride

Afternoon: Cruise to Kom Ombo, visit unique double temple

Evening: Sail to Aswan, dinner on cruise

Cost: USD 23 per person

Day 6: November 29 (Saturday)

Aswan to Hurghada

Morning: Aswan High Dam, Philae Temple (boat ride to island)

Afternoon: Check out from cruise, optional Nubian Village visit

Evening: Flight to Hurghada, check-in to beach resort

Cost: USD 180 per person

Day 7: November 30 (Sunday)

Hurghada Beach Day

Morning: Beach relaxation, optional snorkeling trip to Giftun Island

Afternoon: Water sports (scuba diving, parasailing, quad biking)

Evening: Sunset at beach, explore Hurghada Marina

Cost: USD 70-90 per person

Day 8: December 1 (Monday)

Departure

Morning: Breakfast, check out, last-minute shopping

Afternoon: Flight to Cairo, connect to international flights

Evening: Return flights to Bangalore and Kolkata

Cost: USD 122 per person

📝 Important Tips & Recommendations

💵 Money Matters

  • Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP)
  • 1 USD ≈ 30-31 EGP
  • Carry USD for easier exchange
  • ATMs widely available
  • Keep small bills for tips

🏥 Health & Safety

  • Drink only bottled water
  • Avoid street food initially
  • Carry basic medications
  • Travel insurance mandatory
  • Keep document copies

🕌 Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly at religious sites
  • Remove shoes in mosques
  • Ask before photographing people
  • Bargain at markets (50% start)
  • Learn basic Arabic phrases

📸 Photography

  • Pyramids: Free outside
  • Museums: Camera fee required
  • Tombs: Often prohibited
  • Respect "No Photo" signs
  • Best time: Early morning
